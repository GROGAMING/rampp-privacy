Privacy Policy

Effective 11 May 2026 · Last updated 2 July 2026

This policy explains what data the Rampp mobile app collects, why we collect it, where it is stored, and the rights you have over it. Rampp is provided by James Redmond ("we", "us", "the operator"). If you have questions about anything in this policy, email jredmond135@gmail.com.

If you operate Rampp through a registered business entity, replace "James Redmond" above with the legal entity name and registered address before you submit to the App Store.

1. Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to anyone who installs the Rampp app on iOS or Android, creates an account, or is invited into a team inside the app. It covers data we collect through the app itself. It does not cover third-party services you reach by leaving the app.

2. What we collect

We only collect what the app needs to work. We do not sell data, we do not run advertising trackers, and we do not share data with data brokers.

Account data

  • Email address (used to sign you in via a one-time code).
  • Display name and role inside a team (coach or player), set by you in the app.

Training data you create

  • Session logs and set logs (exercise, weight, reps, RPE, notes you enter).
  • Daily check-ins: self-reported sleep, soreness, energy, mood, and stress ratings.
  • Photos you upload as receipts of completed training (e.g. a picture of the whiteboard, your gym, or your completed work).
  • GPS performance data (e.g. distance covered, top speed, sprint counts) where your coach uploads it from a GPS tracking provider such as StatSports and assigns it to you.

Team data

  • Team memberships, team invite codes you create or join with.
  • Reports and blocks: if you report a post or block another user, we record who reported/blocked whom and the reason text you entered. This is required by Apple's content moderation rules.

Technical data

  • App version, device platform (iOS or Android), and crash diagnostics when the app fails. We use this to fix bugs. It is not linked to advertising profiles.

We do not collect: precise location, contacts, browsing history, health data from Apple Health or Google Fit, biometric data, or financial information.

Notifications

  • If you opt in via the Account screen, Rampp schedules local notifications on your device (daily check-in reminder, supplement reminder). These are scheduled by the operating system on your phone and never travel through our servers. We do not collect a push notification token. We do not send remote push notifications.

RAMPP Nutrition marketing emails (optional)

  • If you tick the "Send me RAMPP Nutrition deals" checkbox at sign-up or in Account, your email address (and your first name, if you've set it) is sent to our email marketing provider Klaviyo (klaviyo.com) and added to a marketing list. You can unsubscribe instantly from any email's footer or by unticking the box in the app.
  • If you do not tick the box, your email is not shared with Klaviyo. We only call Klaviyo when you actively opt in.

4. Legal basis (UK and EU users)

Under UK GDPR and EU GDPR, we rely on:

  • Performance of a contract — to give you the service you signed up for.
  • Consent — for photo uploads and daily check-in data, which you choose to submit each time.
  • Legitimate interests — to keep the app secure, prevent abuse, and fix crashes.

You can withdraw consent at any time by deleting the relevant data in the app, or by deleting your account.

5. Where your data is stored

Rampp is built on Supabase, hosted in the EU-West-1 region (Ireland). Backups stay inside the EU.

Email delivery for sign-in codes goes through Resend (resend.com). Resend processes your email address solely to deliver the code to you.

If you explicitly opt in to marketing emails inside the app (the "RAMPP Nutrition deals" checkbox), your email address and display name are shared with Klaviyo (klaviyo.com), the service we use to send those emails. This only happens when you tick the box, and you can opt out at any time in the app or via the unsubscribe link in any email. If you never opt in, nothing is sent to Klaviyo.

Marketing emails (if you opted in) are sent via Klaviyo (klaviyo.com), which stores your email address and first name to deliver those campaigns. Klaviyo's privacy notice is at klaviyo.com/legal/privacy-notice.

We do not transfer your data outside the European Economic Area, except for marketing emails which use Klaviyo's US-based infrastructure (operating under the EU-US Data Privacy Framework).

6. Who can see your data

  • You can see all of your own data.
  • Your coaches (people who created or co-administer a team you have joined) can see your training data, your daily check-ins, and your photo uploads.
  • Your teammates can see your display name and the posts you make in a team feed.
  • We can access data only when needed to operate the app — for example, to investigate a crash, respond to a content report, or comply with a legal request.

We do not share data with advertisers, analytics networks, or data brokers.

7. How long we keep your data

We keep your data for as long as your account exists. When you delete your account from inside the app (Account → Delete Account):

  • Your set logs, session logs, daily check-ins, photo uploads, team memberships, and profile are deleted from our database.
  • Your authentication record is removed from Supabase Auth.

Backups containing deleted records are rotated out within 30 days.

8. Your rights

If you are in the UK, EU, or another jurisdiction with similar laws, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correct data that is wrong.
  • Delete your account and all associated data (available in-app: Account → Delete Account).
  • Object to or restrict certain processing.
  • Receive an export of your data.
  • Lodge a complaint with your data protection authority. In Ireland that is the Data Protection Commission (dataprotection.ie). In the UK that is the ICO (ico.org.uk).

To use any of these rights, email jredmond135@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.

9. Children

Rampp is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If a coach intends to use Rampp with players under 13, the player's parent or guardian must consent and create the account on the player's behalf. In jurisdictions where the GDPR age is set higher than 13 (for example, 16 in some EU states), the local age applies.

If you believe a child has signed up without parental consent, email jredmond135@gmail.com and we will remove the account.

10. Security

Data in transit is encrypted with TLS. Data at rest in Supabase is encrypted. Row-level security policies in our database restrict every row to its owner and authorised team members.

No system is perfectly secure. If we ever detect a breach affecting your data, we will notify you and the Irish Data Protection Commission within 72 hours as required by GDPR.

11. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy we will update the "Last updated" date at the top and, where appropriate, notify you inside the app. Continuing to use Rampp after a change means you accept the updated policy.
